Case Study

Virtual Bank
Page Redesign

01 Project Overview

Project Type
UX Redesign & Conversion Optimization
Company
MoneyHero — Hong Kong
Timeline
March – April 2022
Team
Product Lead, Cross-functional Stakeholders

02 Problem

With the rise of virtual banks in Hong Kong, MoneyHero needed to establish itself as a trusted comparison platform to drive affiliate conversions. However, the existing WordPress architecture was outdated and inflexible, limiting scalability and content structure.

The page experience overwhelmed users with long, non-mobile-friendly layouts and excessive information. Key CTAs were buried, and the cluttered hierarchy made it difficult to compare complex "Welcome Offers." This increased user frustration, reinforced skepticism, and ultimately led to high drop-off rates.

03 Research and Insights

Focus Group Alignment

I co-hosted a focus group with the Product Lead to gather cross-functional feedback, bringing together perspectives from marketing, content management, and business stakeholders to surface both user-facing and operational pain points.

Affinity Mapping

By organizing stakeholder observations, we uncovered dual-sided issues. On the backend, technical limitations caused inflexible updating systems and duplicated efforts for content managers. On the frontend, users struggled with insufficient imagery, unclear product details, and difficulty comparing offers.

Backend Problem

Inflexible WordPress CMS requiring duplicated manual effort to maintain and update product content across pages

Frontend Problem

Users could not compare "Welcome Offers" effectively due to poor visual hierarchy and buried CTAs in dense text layouts

04 Design Strategy

Designing for Trust

Overcome user skepticism by bringing critical "Security Features" and clear promotional terms to the forefront of the UI, reducing the hesitation users felt about lesser-known virtual bank brands.

Scalable Standardization

Replace the rigid, text-heavy WordPress layouts with a standardized, modular design system (Product Cards) to improve both user scannability and backend content management efficiency.

Mobile-First Retention

Solve the long-page drop-off issue by implementing persistent navigation elements that keep the primary action (conversion) accessible at all times without interrupting the reading flow.

05 Solution

Modular Card UI — Cognitive Load Reduction

Swapped heavy text blocks for scannable, category-based cards. Key metrics (rates, cash amounts) are visually amplified for instant comparison without reading paragraphs.

Visual Logo Filters — Recognition over Recall

Replaced manual scrolling and text-searching with a prominent row of tap-able bank logos. Users can instantly filter the dataset by brand recognition rather than recall.

Standardized CTAs — Conversion Optimization

Upgraded hidden text links to high-contrast, full-width orange action buttons anchored at the bottom of every card, making the conversion point undeniable and predictable.

06 Impact

Delivered a trustworthy, mobile-optimized comparison experience that increased overall user conversion rates for virtual bank affiliate applications.

Significantly decreased page bounce rates on long-form content by introducing the persistent floating navigation bar that kept conversion CTAs always in reach.

Streamlined backend operations, reducing the time required for the marketing team to manage and update promotional offers through the standardized modular card system.
