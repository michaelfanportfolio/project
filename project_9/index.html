Case Study

Multi-Step Car Insurance
Checkout Flow & Self-Serve Dashboard

01 Project Overview

Project Type
Checkout UX & Post-Purchase Experience
Company
MoneyHero and SingSaver
Timeline
2021
Team
Cross-functional teams: Product, Insurance, and Marketing

02 Problem

Car insurance on MoneyHero and SingSaver was the highest-value insurance category by premium, yet it had the worst checkout completion rate. The root cause was a form experience that felt designed for data entry rather than for a human making a significant financial decision.

The multi-step flow required users to manually locate obscure vehicle data (chassis number, engine number), lacked real-time pricing feedback when adjusting coverage options, and provided no self-serve path for post-purchase changes — forcing every amendment through a high-cost customer support phone call.

03 Research and Insights

Complex Vehicle Data Entry

Users struggled to manually locate and input specific vehicle data (e.g., chassis number, engine number), causing immense frustration and mid-funnel drop-offs. Session recordings showed users bouncing to Google to find their vehicle documents, then failing to return to the checkout flow.

No Real-Time Pricing Feedback

Customers lacked visibility into how tweaking their policy (like adjusting excess or adding a named driver) impacted their final premium in real-time, causing hesitation and mistrust. Users were making coverage decisions blind — committing to add-ons without understanding the cost impact until the final summary screen.

Poor Post-Purchase UX

Post-purchase, users found it difficult to update their details, resorting to resource-heavy customer support phone calls instead of digital self-service. This not only degraded the customer experience but generated disproportionate operational costs for a category that had relatively frequent mid-term changes (new named drivers, address updates).

04 Design Strategy

Frictionless Onboarding Architecture

Integrated 3rd-party APIs (Retrieve MyInfo with Singpass) at the very beginning of the flow to automate data entry. This strategic shift moves the user's mindset from "filling out a tedious form" to simply "verifying my information," drastically reducing the initial bounce rate and eliminating the friction point of manual vehicle data lookup.

Progressive Disclosure & Cognitive Load Reduction

Enhanced the UI by breaking down the dense application into 5 digestible steps. By utilizing conditional logic, advanced options (like "Named driver 1" details) only appear when triggered, keeping the interface uncluttered and accelerating decision-making. Each step has a single clear objective, reducing the choice paralysis that plagued the original flat-form design.

05 Solution

End-to-End Conversion Journey

Creates a low-friction, end-to-end conversion journey. It starts with a conversational "Mad-Libs" form to simplify data entry and reduce cognitive load. A clear step-by-step process builds trust and sets expectations. Product tiers (Basic, Mid, Top) make complex policies easy to compare, speeding up decisions. Trust is reinforced through expert tips and social proof, reducing hesitation. FAQs and a glossary address remaining concerns and clarify jargon. The experience minimises effort, builds confidence, and guides users smoothly from exploration to conversion.

5-Step Checkout Flow

Designed a highly scannable, 5-step checkout UI (Driver, Vehicle, Add-ons, Summary, Payment) that standardizes inputs and instantly highlights key policy benefits alongside the total premium. Real-time pricing updates at every step eliminate the black-box anxiety of the previous single-page form.

Self-Serve Policy Dashboard

Created an autonomous "Manage your policy" dashboard form, enabling users to seamlessly submit post-purchase amendment requests directly to the Customer Service team without picking up the phone. Amendment types, required fields, and expected turnaround times are surfaced contextually based on the user's request category.

06 Impact

18%

Reduction in Add-on Drop-off

Reduced user drop-off at the critical "Add-ons" stage through improved information architecture and transparent real-time pricing updates.

15%

Decrease in Support Tickets

Decreased manual customer service tickets by empowering users with a self-serve post-purchase amendment dashboard, directly saving operational resources.

Reduced friction across the full journey, leading to higher conversion and fewer drop-offs. The simplified "Mad-Libs" form and clear 5-step checkout lower cognitive load and speed up completion from landing page to policy purchase.

The Singpass MyInfo integration was later adopted as a standard onboarding pattern across multiple insurance product verticals on both MoneyHero and SingSaver, amplifying the impact of this single design decision across the entire product portfolio.

The self-serve dashboard established a new post-purchase UX standard for the platform, shifting the product team's thinking from "conversion is the finish line" to "the experience continues after purchase."
