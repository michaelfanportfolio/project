Case Study

Travel Insurance Funnel
& Brand Storytelling

01 Project Overview

Project Type
Core Platform Redesign & Brand Storytelling
Company
MoneyHero — Hong Kong
Timeline
January 2019
Team
Cross-functional teams: Product, Insurance, and Marketing

02 Problem

MoneyHero's travel insurance funnel suffered a 35% form abandonment rate. The existing interface was a generic, text-heavy comparison table that felt transactional rather than trustworthy — commoditizing travel insurance in a way that eroded user confidence and failed to differentiate the brand.

Without a distinct visual identity, the page blended in with competitors. Users were overwhelmed by a wall of identical policy rows with no narrative context, no emotional connection, and no clear guidance on what mattered most for their trip. Cognitive overload was driving users away before they ever reached the quote stage.

Abandonment Rate

35%

Users dropping off before completing the insurance quote form

No Brand Identity

0

Distinct visual storytelling or brand differentiation on the existing page

Cognitive Overload

High

Dense comparison tables with no hierarchy caused user confusion and fatigue

03 Research and Insights

Quantitative Data Analysis:

Leveraged Google Analytics and SEO keyword data to understand user search behaviors and identify the most frequently selected travel destinations and insurance options.

Funnel Drop-off Analysis:

Identified that the highest user drop-off occurred when faced with dense, unbranded "Traveller Details" forms before users felt invested in the brand.

Journey Mapping:

Synthesized user flows to find that users needed continuous reassurance throughout the quoting process.

Key Insight: Emotion as a Conversion Driver

Research consistently pointed to one finding: insurance purchase decisions are driven less by rational comparison and more by emotional reassurance. Users needed to feel that MoneyHero understood their travel context before they would trust the platform with a purchase decision.

User Sentiment

"I can't tell if this covers what I actually need for my trip — there's too much information and none of it feels relevant to me."

Business Context

SEO traffic was strong but failing to convert. The drop-off indicated a page-experience problem, not a traffic or product problem — the fix needed to be design-led.

04 Design Strategy

Imagery & Storytelling

Replace the generic table interface with destination-led photography and a narrative-first layout. By surfacing travel imagery that mirrors the user's intended trip, the page builds emotional resonance before presenting policy options — transforming insurance selection from a chore into a natural part of trip planning.

Data-Driven Efficiency

Use funnel analytics to redesign each step for minimum friction. Progressively disclose form fields, pre-fill known traveller details, and surface only the highest-conversion policy attributes. Every UI decision is grounded in drop-off data rather than assumptions about what users need to see.

SEO-Driven Mitigation

Implement a smart redirect strategy to preserve high-value SEO equity during the transition. By mapping legacy URLs to equivalent new pages and surfacing contextually relevant content for each destination query, the redesign would maintain organic traffic while delivering a dramatically improved experience.

05 Solution

Visual Storytelling Interface

Contextual Destination Flow

Sentence-Form Input — Conversational Clarity

Replaced the traditional multi-field form with a sentence-form input pattern: "I'm travelling to [destination] with [number] travellers from [date] to [date]." This approach mirrors natural language, reduces perceived complexity, and keeps users in a trip-planning mindset rather than a form-completion mindset.

Optimised UI Components — Scannability

Policy cards were restructured to surface the three key decision factors — price, coverage limit, and top feature — in a scannable hierarchy. Secondary details are collapsed by default and revealed progressively, reducing the cognitive load that was previously driving abandonment.

Visual Progress Indicator — Momentum Building

A persistent, step-based progress indicator at the top of each funnel screen shows users exactly where they are and how close they are to completion. Combined with micro-animations at each step transition, this created a sense of momentum that counteracted the tendency to abandon partway through.

Smart SEO Redirects

Collaborated deeply with the SEO team to ensure high-intent traffic from Google Search bypassed the initial funnel steps. Users searching specific keywords were redirected straight to a pre-configured, personalized result page, eliminating unnecessary clicks.

06 Impact

28%

Reduction in Form Abandonment

The sentence-form input and progressive disclosure dramatically reduced the abandonment rate that prompted this redesign project.

15%

Increase in Conversions

More users completed the purchase flow following the redesign, directly attributable to improved clarity and emotional engagement throughout the funnel.

Established MoneyHero's first distinctive brand presence in the travel insurance vertical through destination-led visual storytelling, differentiating the platform from commodity comparison competitors.

The SEO redirect strategy successfully preserved organic traffic equity across the 200+ legacy URLs during the platform migration, with no significant drop in search-driven sessions post-launch.

The sentence-form input pattern and visual progress indicator became a foundational design pattern adopted across other MoneyHero insurance funnels in subsequent quarters.
