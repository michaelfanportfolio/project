MoneyHero App eKYC & TU Credit Score Integration

01 Project Overview

Project Type
Mobile App Product Design, FinTech Conversion Optimization, Identity Verification (eKYC)
Company
MoneyHero
Team
Product Managers, Business Development, Marketing, Engineering
Timeline
2022

02 Problem

In 2022, MoneyHero offered over 1,500 financial products and reached an impressive 7.8 million monthly unique users. However, massive top-of-funnel traffic did not automatically guarantee revenue.

The existing web-centric approach facilitated a "blind application" process. Users often applied for unsuitable products without knowing their credit eligibility upfront, resulting in high rejection rates, wasted applications, and user frustration. The business required a secure, scalable solution to transition users from awareness to deterministic matching, ultimately increasing the click-to-approval conversion rate while satisfying banking partners' demands for high-quality applicants.

03 Research and Insights

User Personas:

We identified distinct behavioral profiles driving the product strategy:

  • Andy (The Optimizer): A 40-year-old bank manager juggling a tight cash flow. He requires absolute speed, secure biometric platforms, and personalized, high-probability loan matching.
  • Alex (The Subprime Rehabilitator): A 30-something salesman with a history of loan rejections. He needs actionable tracking tools to improve his score for future milestones (like buying a home).
  • Angie (The Deal Hunter): A savvy spender focused on campaign rewards and promotions.

Stakeholder Alignment:

  • Business Development: Banks prioritize high-quality applicants. A verified TransUnion (TU) credit score reassures lenders, accelerating approvals and increasing the success rate of MoneyHero’s recommendations.
  • Marketing: Users inherently want access to their credit scores for financial planning (negotiating terms, avoiding application mistakes, protecting against fraud). The lack of an accessible, native tool created a gap in their financial journey.

04 Design Strategy

The Hybrid Model (App-First ecosystem):

We architected a hybrid pipeline. The Web platform functions as the top-of-funnel marketing and education hub. The Native App handles deep authentication, securely housing the TU credit score, eKYC document uploads, and personalized push notifications.

Security & Trust Parity:

Because TU credit reports are highly sensitive, the app environment leverages end-to-end encrypted transfer directly to TU’s backend, reinforced by Face ID / Touch ID. Every input field was designed to contextualize this trust, explaining why data is required and how it is encrypted.

Integrates key competitor insights:

A compliance-first approach with gamified engagement. Front-loading eKYC during the six-step onboarding establishes immediate trust and boosts completion rates. Simultaneously, upfront visualized rewards tied to TransUnion credit scores and scenario-based education create a motivating, transparent journey that drives active user retention.

05 Solution

A Reward-Driven, Transparent Onboarding Flow

The proposed onboarding solution translates our strategy into a seamless user interface. It begins with a clear value proposition carousel, leading immediately into an educational overview of core features. To drive early eKYC completion, we integrated a prominent, upfront reward banner (HK$50 voucher). The subsequent identity verification screen prioritizes transparency, outlining the three-step process while addressing user concerns through embedded FAQs on data privacy and security.

Guided Document Scanning:

Broken down the HKID scanning process into hyper-focused steps. Added visual cues (e.g., "Avoid Table Surface") and an optional audio guide to support accessibility and reduce scan failures.

Account Review Summary:

Implemented a structured review stage prior to final submission. This builds user confidence, ensures data accuracy, and drastically lowers the likelihood of application rejection due to typos.

Deterministic Matching Dashboard:

Integrated the TU API to show credit eligibility upfront. By displaying a verified score, the dashboard filters out unsuitable products early, shortens the decision path, and actively guides users toward credit cards and loans with the highest approval odds.

06 Impact

Increased Conversion Rate: By eliminating the guesswork of loan applications, users focused on viable options, significantly improving application completion rates and click-to-approval metrics.

Strengthened Provider Trust: Delivering pre-vetted applicants with verified TU scores made banking partners substantially more willing to approve applications.

Improved User Retention: Consolidating TU score tracking, personalized product matching, and campaign rewards into a single, secure app environment fostered daily engagement and long-term financial health improvement.
